biography

2003–2008: Career Beginnings

Kendrick Lamar started his career while attending Centennial High School, adopting the stage name K.Dot. He gained attention for his freestyling and battle rapping, leading to his first mixtape, Youngest Head Nigga in Charge (2004). This project caught the interest of Top Dawg Entertainment (TDE) founder Anthony "Top Dawg" Tiffith, who signed Lamar in 2005. Lamar formed the hip hop supergroup Black Hippy with TDE labelmates Jay Rock, Ab-Soul, and Schoolboy Q.

2009–2011: Overly Dedicated and Section.80

Lamar released C4 (2009), a tribute to Lil Wayne’s Tha Carter III. He then changed his stage name from K.Dot to Kendrick Lamar and released the Kendrick Lamar EP (2009), which marked a shift in his artistry. His mixtape Overly Dedicated (2010) peaked at number 72 on the Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart. Lamar's debut studio album, Section.80 (2011), received critical acclaim and included the single "HiiiPower."

2012–2013: Good Kid, M.A.A.D City

Lamar's major label debut, Good Kid, M.A.A.D City (2012), was a critical and commercial success, debuting at number two on the Billboard 200 and being certified platinum. The album included hits like "Swimming Pools (Drank)" and "Bitch, Don’t Kill My Vibe." Lamar was nominated for seven Grammy Awards in 2014, although he did not win any, which was widely considered a snub.

2014–2016: To Pimp a Butterfly and Untitled Unmastered

To Pimp a Butterfly (2015) incorporated jazz, funk, and soul, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200. It included the singles "The Blacker the Berry," "King Kunta," and "Alright." Lamar won five Grammy Awards in 2016, including Best Rap Album. He released Untitled Unmastered (2016), a compilation of unreleased tracks from the To Pimp a Butterfly sessions, which also debuted at number one.

2017–2018: DAMN. and the Pulitzer Prize

Lamar's fourth studio album, DAMN. (2017), featured hit singles like "HUMBLE.," "DNA.," and "LOYALTY." The album earned him the Pulitzer Prize for Music in 2018, making him the first non-classical or jazz artist to win the award. DAMN. also won Best Rap Album at the Grammy Awards.

2019–Present: Continued Success and Upcoming Projects

Lamar contributed to the soundtrack for the film Black Panther (2018), which was critically acclaimed and received several awards. In 2021, he announced the forthcoming release of his final TDE album, Mr. Morale & the Big Steppers (2022), which continued to receive critical acclaim. Lamar remains an influential figure in hip hop and continues to work on new projects.
